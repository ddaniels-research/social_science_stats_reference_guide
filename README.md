**Social Science Statistics Reference Guide**

A comprehensive, decision-tree-based guide for choosing and implementing statistical analyses in social science research. This resource is designed for graduate students and early-career researchers to navigate the process of selecting the correct test, checking assumptions, and reporting results in APA format.

## Key Features
Decision-Tree Approach: A structured flow to help you arrive at the right test based on your research question, variable types, and measurement levels.
35+ Statistical Methods: Detailed coverage ranging from basic t-tests to advanced methods like Structural Equation Modeling (SEM) and Multilevel Modeling (HLM).
Practical R Code: Ready-to-use R implementation examples for every test mentioned in the guide. 
APA Reporting Templates: Standardized templates to help you report your findings clearly and accurately in academic writing.
Supplementary Material: Comprehensive sections on assumption-checking, effect size interpretation, and troubleshooting violated assumptions.

## Guide Structure### 
### Part 1: Decision-Tree FlowA walkthrough of five major research branches to identify the appropriate analysis:
Comparing Groups: t-tests, ANOVA, and non-parametric alternatives.
Examining Relationships: Pearson and Spearman correlations, Chi-square, and Partial correlations.
Predicting Outcomes: Linear, Logistic, and Poisson regression.
Examining Structure: Exploratory and Confirmatory Factor Analysis (EFA/CFA).
Advanced Methods: Mediation, Moderation, HLM, and SEM.

### Part 2: Categorical Quick-Reference
Tests grouped by family (T-Test, ANOVA, Regression, etc.) for fast lookup when you already know the required method.

### Part 3: Supplementary Material
Tools for ensuring statistical rigor, including:
Assumption-Checking: Guides for normality, homogeneity of variance, and multicollinearity.
Effect Size Table: Benchmarks for Cohen's $d$, $\eta^2$, $R^2$, and more.
Troubleshooting: Practical "What to Do" steps when data fails to meet parametric assumptions.

## Usage
Each entry in this guide follows a consistent, easy-to-digest template:
Plain-language explanation of what the test does.
Concrete social science example and intuitive analogy.
Statistical formula with defined symbols.
R Implementation using base R or specialized packages (e.g., lavaan, ez, car).
APA Reporting Template with example values.

## About the Author
This guide was developed as a reference for researchers in psychology, sociology, and related fields to bridge the gap between theoretical statistics and practical data analysis.
